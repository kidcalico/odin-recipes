Kettle corn is a nostalgic treat that balances sweet and salty in every crunchy handful. This version gives it a wholesome twist by using coconut sugar instead of refined white sugar, adding a subtle caramel flavor that pairs beautifully with the popcorn’s light crunch. Made on the stovetop with just a few pantry staples, it’s quick, vegan, and healthier than most packaged snacksâ€”-perfect for movie nights or a sweet-savory pick-me-up.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (100g) popcorn kernels
  • 3 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup (65g) coconut sugar
  • Sea salt to taste

Recipe

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat. Drop in 2-3 kernels and cover with a lid.
  2. Once the test kernels pop, add the remaining popcorn kernels and sprinkle coconut sugar evenly over the top. Stir quickly to coat, then cover with the lid.
  3. Shake the pot gently every few seconds to keep the sugar from burning and to move unpopped kernels to the bottom.
  4. When popping lows to 1-2 seconds between pops, remove from heat. Immediately sprinkle with salt and give the pot a final shake.
  5. Allow popcorn to cool slightly before serving (it’s hot!)
