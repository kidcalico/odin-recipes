Banana Bread

Home


Nothing beats a warm slice of banana bread. Photo by Teo Do Rio on Unsplash

Banana bread is a cozy, feel-good classic that’s perfect for using up ripe bananas and filling your kitchen with the comforting smell of fresh baking. This vegan version swaps eggs for a flaxseed mixture and skips refined sugar, letting the natural sweetness of bananas shine through. The addition of walnuts brings a lovely crunch and nutty depth, making each slice hearty and satisfying while staying wholesome. It’s a healthier twist on a favorite that still feels indulgent.

  • 3 medium ripe bananas (~300g) mashed
  • 2 tbsp ground flaxseed + 6 tbsp water (for flax egg)
  • 1/3 cup (80mL) melted coconut oil (or avocado oil)
  • 1/4 cup (60mL) maple syrup or date syrup (optional for added sweetness)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose/whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup (60g) chopped walnuts
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease or line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together ground flaxseed and water. Let sit 5-10 minutes to thicken.
  3. In a large bowl, mash bananas until smooth. Whisk in flax egg, oil, syrup (if using), and vanilla.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix flour, baking soda, cinnamon and salt.
  5. Gently fold dry ingredients into wet until just combined--don't overmix. Stir in walnuts.
  6. Pour batter into loaf pan and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 45-55 minutes. Stick it with a fork or a toothpick, if it comes out clean, it's finished!
  8. Let it cool in the pan for ten minutes, then let it cool a little longer on a wire rack before slicing.
  9. Enjoy your fresh banana bread, try it toasted plain or with some nut butter!
