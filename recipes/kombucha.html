Kombucha is a tangy, lightly fizzy fermented tea that’s surprisingly easy to make at home. Using just tea, sugar, water, and a SCOBY (a living culture of bacteria and yeast), you can brew a refreshing drink that balances sweet and tart flavors while developing natural carbonation. The process is simple: brew sweet tea, add the SCOBY, and let time and fermentation do the work. In about a week, you’ll have your own homemade kombucha that can be enjoyed plain or flavored with fruit, herbs, or spices for a personalized twist.

Ingredients

  • 16 cups (1 gallon/~3.8 liters) water
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 8 black or green teabags or two tbsp loose tea
  • 1 SCOBY
  • Large jar (capable of holding 4 quarts/3.8 liters)

Recipe

  1. Boil about 4 cups of water. Add tea bags and sugar, stir until sugar dissolves. Let it steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Remove tea bags and pour sweet tea into a clean glass jar. Add the rest of the water to cool it down.
  3. When tea is room temperature, with clean hands, add SCOBY to the jar.
  4. Cover the jar with a breathable cloth secured with a rubber band.
  5. Now the exciting part! Let it ferment at room temperature (68-85°F or 18-29°C) for 7-10 days, tasting periodically until it reaches your preferred balance of sweet and tart.
  6. Remove the SCOBY, placing it in a separate container to save it for the next batch.
  7. Using a clean siphon or ladle and a funnel, add the fermented Kombucha into clean bottles. You can also add fruit, ginger or herbs to flavor. Be careful at first with super sweet fruit as it can cause over carbonation.
  8. Seal the bottles and let them sit in the dark for two or three days to carbonate.
